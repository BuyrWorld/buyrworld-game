# BuyrWorld Comprehensive Independent Game Audit

**Context:** All evaluation was conducted from the perspective of a multidisciplinary team (lead game designer, creative director, product manager, UX researcher, MMORPG designer, economy designer, live service director, Steam publishing consultant, VC games investor and an experienced gamer with no prior exposure to BuyrWorld).  The game was played through its tutorial, core loop and early systems in July 2026 using the latest build at [BuyrWorld.com](https://www.buyrworld.com).  Citations refer to observations and externally-sourced information about comparable games.

## Phase 3 — Deep Systems Review

Here we audit each major system found in BuyrWorld, highlight what works, what does not, and how AAA studios would improve it.

### Mining System
- **What works:** The mining tab offers multiple ore types (Iron, Copper, Coal, etc.) requiring specific levels.  Clicking a task initiates an auto‑mining bar and yields resources and XP. The warehouse displays resource counts.  The system introduces players to resource gating and progression.  XP levels provide long‑term goals.
- **What doesn’t:** The activity is passive; players watch a timer fill repeatedly.  There are no decisions (e.g., which rock to prioritise), no risk (no stamina or tool durability), and no spatial gameplay (clicking the actual ore in the scene is pointless).  Mining visuals are repetitive.  There is no random event or bonus to keep players alert.
- **AAA improvements:** Introduce interactive mining: player must click or hold to hit ore, avoid hazards, or choose between ore nodes with different yields.  Add tool upgrades, skill specialisations (e.g., speed vs. quality), and random events (geodes, gems).  Sound and vibration feedback should make mining tactile.  Visuals could show the character moving within a mine.

### Steelworks/Smelting
- **What works:** Smelting tasks convert raw ore into bars at fixed rates.  The cost ratio (e.g., 3 Iron Ore → 1 Iron Bar) encourages planning.  XP is awarded; tasks are gated by level.  The interface is clear about resource costs.
- **What doesn’t:** Again, purely timer‑based; no player agency.  No risk of failure or over‑smelting.  Visuals show a static furnace.  The system is isolated; smelting doesn’t interact with other players or market supply/demand.
- **AAA improvements:** Add mini‑games (timing or temperature control), chances for quality variants (refined bars), and tool upgrades.  Visuals could show ores pouring into furnaces.  Provide synergy with companions or research that reduces fuel cost.  Offer auto‑queueing for continuous production and offline progress.

### Manufacturing
- **What works:** Converting bars into components (e.g., brackets) adds a second layer of production.  This demonstrates a basic supply chain.  XP and level gating continue.
- **What doesn’t:** Pressing brackets is another timer without decisions or variation.  There is no craft queue management; tasks must be clicked manually.  Only a few recipes exist; the progression feels thin.
- **AAA improvements:** Introduce multiple recipes with different inputs and outputs; allow players to choose production priorities.  Add craft queues, multi‑step production lines and quality management.  Visuals should show machines operating.  Provide blueprint systems akin to *Factorio* where players design assembly lines; AAA games like *Satisfactory* allow complex factory construction【377722659451727†L213-L233】.

### Woodcutting
- **What works:** Additional resource system beyond mining.  New skill XP and tasks (Pine, Oak, etc.).  Introduces longer timers and varied yields【106230746050670†screenshot】.
- **What doesn’t:** Same issues as mining—passive, no interplay with environment.  The wood has few uses; logs are only used later in manufacturing or trade.  Visuals are static (trees don’t fall).  No environmental effect like deforestation or replanting.
- **AAA improvements:** Add mini‑game or physical cutting interactions.  Introduce tree growth cycles and reforestation, requiring management of forest sustainability.  Provide more uses for wood (construction, tools).  Integrate with housing system where players craft buildings.

### Fishing
- **What works:** Offers variety; tasks for different fish with level gating【946959369200416†screenshot】.  Adds food resources and another skill.
- **What doesn’t:** Passive progress bar again.  Fish appear as generic icons; there is no fishing rod or animations.  There is no risk or chance of catching rare fish, no dynamic day/night cycles.
- **AAA improvements:** Use a timing mini‑game or interactive fishing (hooking and reeling).  Introduce weather, seasons, fish rarity and baits.  Fish could be used for cooking or trading with other players, similar to *RuneScape* where fishing supports cooking and combat【291543952781440†L222-L233】.

### Trade/Market
- **What works:** Market offers NPCs who sell and buy resources at varying prices【920448371125008†screenshot】.  Each vendor specialises (ore broker, scrap dealer, fishmonger, homeware vendor).  Buying and selling introduces economic decisions.
- **What doesn’t:** Prices appear static or rarely change; there is no supply/demand dynamic.  Only NPC trading exists—no player‑driven economy like in *EVE Online* where supply/demand is player‑controlled【285527725163065†L204-L249】.  UI is cluttered and difficult to navigate; each vendor requires scrolling.  There is no indicator for price history or profit potential.
- **AAA improvements:** Implement dynamic pricing based on player trades and global events.  Add a graph to track price trends.  Create a central auction house for player‑to‑player trading, enabling speculation and arbitrage akin to *EVE*’s market.  Provide quick‑sell and bulk‑buy features with confirm dialogues.

### Contracts (Logistics)
- **What works:** Contracts require delivering specific items for coins and XP【897445715652612†screenshot】.  The ability to re‑tender for 25 coins introduces choice.  Rewards scale with difficulty.  This system adds purpose to production.
- **What doesn’t:** Contracts are predetermined; there is no negotiation or risk.  Deliveries happen instantly by clicking deliver; there is no transport or logistics mini‑game.  The contract variety is limited to early items; later goods may be limited.
- **AAA improvements:** Add delivery mechanics—players must allocate vehicles, choose routes, handle capacity and travel time.  Introduce penalties for late delivery.  Provide a contract board with player‑generated orders.  Add reputation with different factions depending on contract fulfilment.

### Upgrades
- **What works:** Town Hall offers upgrades that improve production speeds or contract payouts【766587002826250†screenshot】.  Cost scales moderately; provides progression goal.
- **What doesn’t:** The list is static and small.  There is no tech tree or visual representation of upgrades.  Upgrades feel like passive number boosts rather than altering gameplay.
- **AAA improvements:** Develop a branching technology tree with choices that affect strategy (e.g., efficiency vs. capacity).  Represent upgrades visually in the world (bigger furnaces, improved workshops).  Provide synergy and diminishing returns to encourage diversification.

### Companions
- **What works:** Companion barn shows empty slots for logistic companions【824366037474132†screenshot】.  Suggests upcoming feature where companions help deliver or produce items.
- **What doesn’t:** Currently empty; no actual system.  UI just shows placeholder boxes.  Without details players are confused.
- **AAA improvements:** Add recruitable companions with unique abilities (e.g., bonus XP, faster deliveries).  Make them level up and bond through quests.  Provide a collection and rarity system similar to gacha games to monetise cosmetic skins.

### Currencies & Economy
- **What works:** Single currency (coins) used for contracts and upgrades.  XP builds skill levels.  Resources can be sold for coins. | The economy is simple and easy to grasp.
- **What doesn’t:** No premium currency or soft/hard currency separation.  Without multiple currencies the monetisation potential is limited.  No sinks for coins aside from upgrades and re‑tendering; risk of inflation.  Without dynamic pricing the economy feels static.
- **AAA improvements:** Introduce multiple currencies (e.g., gold coins, gems).  Implement sinks such as maintenance costs, taxes, or property upkeep.  Provide exclusive cosmetics purchasable with premium currency.  Balanced economic design would increase longevity.

### Levels & Skills
- **What works:** Each activity has its own skill level (e.g., Mining 1/99).  XP progression triggers unlocks for new tasks and awards. | Level gating provides long‑term goals and encourages players to try new activities.
- **What doesn’t:** Leveling is linear and slow; there are no skill perks or specialisations.  No synergy between skills.  No sense of character growth outside of numbers.
- **AAA improvements:** Add talent trees allowing players to choose specialisations (e.g., faster mining vs. chance for double ore).  Provide synergy bonuses for balanced play.  Visual and audio cues for level‑ups would increase satisfaction.  Offer global ranking leaderboards and achievements.

### Quests & Achievements
- **What works:** A few early awards (“First Swing”, “Stay Frosty”) pop up in the log. | Recognises milestones and encourages progression.
- **What doesn’t:** Awards are not visible in a dedicated achievements screen; players cannot track progress.  No quests beyond the tutorial.  Achievements do not reward meaningful bonuses. |  
- **AAA improvements:** Implement a quest journal with daily, weekly and story quests.  Provide achievements with cosmetic or currency rewards.  Use badges and titles to encourage competition.

### Inventory & Collections
- **What works:** Warehouse lists resources with counts. | Clear and constant view of resource inventory.
- **What doesn’t:** No concept of storage capacity; resources accumulate infinitely.  No item details or quality.  There is no personal inventory for equipment, clothing or cosmetics. |
- **AAA improvements:** Add storage limits requiring warehouse upgrades.  Introduce item quality (e.g., common → legendary) for manufactured goods.  Provide a character inventory for equipment, tools, clothing and decorative items.

### Research & Automation
- **What works:** None currently. |
- **What doesn’t:** There is no research tree or automation beyond auto‑completion of tasks.  Players cannot queue tasks or run processes offline.
- **AAA improvements:** Implement automation (e.g., queue tasks, auto‑deliver).  Add research projects that unlock new recipes or efficiency.  Provide drones or conveyors like *Factorio*【525925262801514†L223-L236】.

### Prestige/Idle Mechanics
- **What works:** Basic idle progress while active. |
- **What doesn’t:** There is no prestige system to reset progress for permanent multipliers.  Offline progress halts when the game is closed.
- **AAA improvements:** Introduce a prestige/ascension system similar to *Cookie Clicker* where resetting provides permanent bonuses and encourages replay【306593811335686†L190-L233】.  Provide idle offline progress with diminishing returns to encourage returning to the game.

### Multiplayer Potential
- **What works:** None; currently single‑player. |
- **What doesn’t:** No chat, co‑op or competition.  Without social features retention may suffer.
- **AAA improvements:** Add leaderboards (e.g., highest XP, richest players), guilds with group objectives, trading between players, and cooperative building projects.  Taking inspiration from *EVE Online*’s player‑driven economy and political alliances could make the game socially engaging【285527725163065†L204-L249】.

### Leaderboards & Social
- **What works:** None. |
- **What doesn’t:** No ranking or social sharing. |
- **AAA improvements:** Introduce leaderboards for each skill, contract deliveries, wealth and prestige.  Provide optional login via social accounts to share progress.  Add chat and friend lists to encourage community.

### Events & Seasonal Content
- **What works:** Not implemented. |
- **What doesn’t:** There are no weekly events, limited‑time contracts or seasonal themes. |
- **AAA improvements:** Create seasonal events (e.g., winter festival) with limited‑time resources and rewards.  Offer holiday‑themed contracts and cosmetics.  Live‑service games like *RuneScape* run seasonal events that boost engagement【291543952781440†L222-L233】.

### Daily Rewards & Monetisation
- **What works:** None. |
- **What doesn’t:** No daily login rewards, premium currency, or ad watch reward.  Without monetisation systems the game cannot generate revenue. |
- **AAA improvements:** Add daily login streak rewards and rotating shop with premium items.  Offer optional ads for speed boosts on mobile.  Introduce battle pass with tiers and cosmetic rewards.

### Future Scalability
- **What works:** Modular system (new tabs for new industries). | The game can expand to more resource types, industries, and features.
- **What doesn’t:** The underlying architecture may not support large server concurrency or multiplayer.  Without code optimisation or network features, scaling to thousands of players will be challenging.
- **AAA improvements:** Rebuild the backend to support persistent worlds, player synchronisation and data storage.  Use scalable cloud infrastructure.  Plan content updates and expansions to keep players engaged.

## Phase 4 — User Experience Review

A thorough UX audit reveals friction points and opportunities across devices.

| Area | Evaluation & friction | Suggestions |
|---|---|---|
| **Navigation** | Navigation bar uses small pixel icons with no tooltips.  Players must guess what each symbol means.  Switching between tabs resets the scroll position of the sidebar. | Add text labels or hover tooltips; increase icon size; group related tabs (e.g., resource gathering vs. processing).  Provide breadcrumb or highlight for current tab.
| **Buttons** | Buttons are small and use inconsistent styling (some appear as text links).  Click targets may be too small for mobile. | Standardise button styles; increase size for mobile; add hover and pressed states.
| **Visual hierarchy** | The most important information (objectives, tasks, resources) competes with decorative scene.  The log at the bottom draws attention away from tasks. | Use colour contrast and sizing to emphasise the objective; minimise decorative background; allow collapsing of log.
| **Information overload** | Multiple tabs are available from the start; players are overwhelmed.  Some tasks require resources not yet introduced. | Lock certain tabs until players reach appropriate levels; gradually introduce systems; provide guided tours.
| **Empty screens** | Companions tab shows empty boxes with no explanation【824366037474132†screenshot】.  The fishing or woodcutting screens show idle backgrounds without interactive elements. | Add explanatory text for locked features; use placeholder art or silhouettes with “coming soon” text.
| **Accessibility** | No options for font scaling, colour‑blind mode or key remapping. | Provide settings for font size, high contrast, and alternative input.  Ensure UI elements meet WCAG guidelines.
| **Mobile compatibility** | On smaller screens some text and icons would be tiny.  There is no responsive design; the fixed layout may require zooming. | Implement responsive layout; adapt controls for touch (larger buttons, drag gestures).  Offer a native mobile app for better performance and offline progress.
| **Desktop experience** | Works in browser but lacks fullscreen and windowed options.  No keyboard shortcuts for efficiency. | Provide fullscreen toggle; implement keyboard hotkeys for switching tabs and starting tasks.

| **Colour palette** | Soft pastel colours suit the theme but lack contrast for UI elements.  There is no consistent colour for interactive objects. | Define a clear primary accent colour; ensure call‑to‑action buttons stand out.  Use icons and colours consistently for resource types.
| **Responsiveness** | The game has minimal delay when clicking tasks.  However, some UI elements update slowly (e.g., resource counts update after bar finishes). | Provide immediate feedback on clicks; animate resource increments; ensure tasks can be queued.

| **Menus** | No main menu or settings; the only menu is the tab bar.  There is no pause or save button. | Add a main menu with options (settings, achievements, exit).  Display save progress status and sync across devices.
| **Settings** | Non‑existent. | Implement settings for audio, graphics quality, notifications and account management.
| **Saving** | It seems to auto‑save but there is no indicator; uncertain if progress persists across devices. | Provide explicit save button and auto‑save feedback; allow cloud save and login.

## Phase 5 — Emotional Journey

The team documented how they felt at various points.

1. **Curious** — Starting the game we were curious about the premise and what the systems would involve.  The presence of multiple tabs suggested depth.
2. **Confused** — The lack of labels and narrative made the world feel confusing.  We spent time figuring out which icons corresponded to which activity and where to click to mine.  The first contract required items we hadn’t produced yet.
3. **Excited** — Completing tasks and watching numbers grow triggered mild excitement.  Unlocking the Steelworks and pressing brackets felt like progress.  The first contract delivery gave a satisfying chunk of coins and XP【897445715652612†screenshot】.
4. **Rewarded** — Achievements like “First Swing” popped up, giving a sense of reward.  The upgrade list promised future improvements.
5. **Lost** — After the tutorial, the game left us to explore.  Without guidance on upgrades or new systems we felt aimless.  We tried woodcutting and fishing to regain direction【106230746050670†screenshot】【946959369200416†screenshot】.
6. **Motivated** — Discovering the market and upgrades rekindled motivation.  Planning supply chains (ore → bars → brackets → contracts) gave us goals.
7. **Bored** — The repetitive timers and lack of interactivity set in.  Without audio or varied visuals the experience became monotonous.  We started multi‑tasking while waiting.
8. **Frustrated** — Searching for hidden features or understanding price differences at the market was frustrating.  The game did not clearly explain upgrade benefits or companion system; some tabs felt unfinished.

Overall, the emotional journey started with curiosity, gained mild excitement and reward, but quickly plateaued into boredom and occasional frustration due to lack of depth and guidance.

## Phase 7 — Potential
